Environmental Laws in the Philippines:
Historical Timeline

Early conservation efforts were largely piecemeal, focusing on specific resources (forestry, etc.) and often driven by economic rather than holistic environmental concerns. Lack of comprehensive legislation.

Growing awareness of environmental issues led to the creation of the Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR) in 1978, marking a significant shift towards centralized environmental management.

Passage of comprehensive laws addressing air, water, and waste challenges.

  • Clean Air Act (RA 8749)
  • Clean Water Act (RA 9275)
  • Ecological Solid Waste Management Act (RA 9003)

Focus on enforcement, climate change adaptation, and legal precedents.

  • Climate Change Act (RA 9729)
